

                                                                                     CASE STUDY - CYBERCRIME


Cyberbullying has become a growing concern in the digital age, especially among students who actively use social media platforms. It involves using technology to harass, threaten, or humiliate someone online. A notable case is that of Riya, a 16-year-old student who enjoyed sharing her thoughts and photos on social media. Unfortunately, a group of her classmates misused this by creating a fake account in her name. They uploaded edited images and posted offensive and misleading content, which quickly spread among other students in her school.

As the situation worsened, Riya began receiving abusive messages and was constantly ridiculed by her peers. This not only affected her academic performance but also had a serious impact on her emotional well-being. She became anxious, lost self-confidence, and started avoiding school and social interactions. The fear of being judged and humiliated made her feel isolated and helpless. Cyberbullying, in such cases, can lead to long-term psychological effects if not addressed properly.

Realizing the seriousness of the issue, Riya finally shared her problem with her parents and a trusted teacher. Immediate action was taken by reporting the fake account, which was eventually removed. The school authorities identified the students responsible and took strict disciplinary measures against them. Additionally, the school conducted awareness programs to educate students about digital ethics, responsible behavior, and the consequences of cyberbullying.

With continuous support from her family and teachers, Riya gradually regained her confidence and returned to her normal routine. This case clearly highlights that cyberbullying is a serious issue that requires prompt action, awareness, and collective responsibility. It also emphasizes the importance of creating a safe and respectful online environment for everyone.
